Mental Illness: The Research on Developmental Trauma - a guide

Search Methods

"Developmental trauma refers to exposure to multiple, cumulative traumatic events, usually of an interpersonal nature, during childhood which results in developmentally adverse consequences." (van der Kolk, 2005, Busuttil, 2009, as cited in Gregorowsk & Seedat, 2013)

Developmentally adverse consequences can include but is not limited to delays in physical, social, behavioural, and cognitive development.

Search Strategies, and why you should apply them

What is a search strategy?

A search strategy is the process you use to look for information, using specific combinations of words and phrases and restricting the results to provide more precise answers to your question.

What are all the below terms?

Terms are words that are related to the topic, and will help pull up information on the topic.

Subject headings are the terms that specific databases use to find results based on the given topic.

Dewey Decimal call numbers are the numbers and letters that let you find books on the given topic at libraries that use the Dewey Decimal system. Likewise, Library of Congress call numbers let you find books on the given topic at libraries that use the Library of Congress system. Typically, larger libraries in the USA use Library of Congress numbers isntead of Dewey Decimal.

Boolean search expressions are a special type of search term that restricts the information pulled up further than just with subject headings or individual terms. They combine terms with commands like AND, OR, and NOT. In some databases, this can be done with the advanced search option and you can fill it in with the text boxes and buttons, and for others, you type it in manually like is shown here.

How are they used?

On the library catalog, the phrases listed under Boolean Expressions can be used directly. On databases, many search options are available through choosing Advanced Search, which allows you to put in the Subject Headings or restrict by publish date. Additionally, combining Subject Headings with specific terms will allow for more specific results, including terms related to your interest. When looking for information at a library in person, the call numbers can be used to find the subject you are looking for.
